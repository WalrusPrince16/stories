PART II

Walker: Part Two In the months following Walker’s selfish sacrifice to the Plague Heart, the already hopeless communities that dotted the dead land were left with nothing while the Plagued Hordes grew in size and strength. Soon the countryside where Walker built his once admirable community was nothing more than a wasteland full of struggling scavengers with no future before them. A blackish-red skin-like film protruding with thick veins covered the earth for miles around the slowly beating Heart. Towers of flesh and bone loomed over the barren landscape, touching the sky and emitting blood-filled clouds of vapor that tinted the clouds a sickly pink color.

At the edge of the bloodied land atop a high clifftop, the last remains of Walker’s settlement lay, now just a withering shack, inhabited by just three survivors who had evaded his slaughter. They were all young, being two men and a woman, on the brink of desperation and living on rations that left them faint. Plagued blood dripped steadily through the shattered roof of their shack, pooling into simmering puddles before evaporating in the scorching heat of the summer’s fading daylight. The youngest of the three, Becki, sat slumped against the wall, unable to move from a wrecking fatigue brought on by hours in sun’s rays with nothing but poorly filtered water to drink. Outside, she could see the men in the middle of piling the bodies of the plagued horde into a deep trench that was nearly overflowing with glistening red bodies covered in skin that rippled like water as it melted away in their final deaths. The red-tinted sun gave them just enough light to complete their morbid task before the bodies’ flesh began to melt into a mucous-like fluid that left their skeletons charred and disjointed before sinking further into the blood-filled trench’s depths.

Wearily, the others entered the building and wiped their bloody hands on a matted towel before collapsing across the room from her, unable to stand any longer. They sat in silence for some time before, afraid that the slightest noise would draw another horde. Nothing but the distant moans of the flesh towers in the plagued lands beyond their settlement could be heard until Becki spoke in a soft fearful voice “Was that the last of them?” Her light-blue eyes darted around to each hole and crevice in the walls in dreadful anticipation. “I think so” sighed Joel, the oldest of the group, a muscular man in his mid-twenties, as he lay his hunting rifle against the wall. Joel had taken up the role of leader of their small group after Walker’s betrayal, having survived his slaughter at the infirmary on that night months ago. “I don’t think I can do this much longer” Becki said as she gazed over her fresh wounds, her voice a mix of hopelessness and a deep sadness. “We’re staying here. There’s not a single settlement in miles that wouldn’t shoot us the second they find out where we came from. I just need more time and we’ll have a working radio to call for help from out of state.” Joel replied sternly. “You think I don’t know that? Walker murdered my parents in front of me! We just need to go far away, I don’t care where, I just can’t live like this anymore, this is worse than being dead” Becki lowered her head, a tear running down her paled cheek. Andrew laid an arm on her shoulder comfortingly, trying his hardest to hide his own sorrow. “We’ll be gone soon. I swore I’d get us out of this place as soon as I could” Joel rose to his feet, steadying himself on weak legs. “Andrew, let’s get the grill going before it gets dark.” Andrew rose to his feet reluctantly, leaving Becki on her own as he exited the dwelling with Joel through a door barely hanging on its hinges. He peaked back through the door, “Don’t worry, this one’s… newer than the last one.” Becki’s sadness was soon overtaken with revulsion as a nauseating aroma akin to burning plastic and sulfur filled the room. Noxious fumes of pinkish hues seeped in through the holes in the walls for several minutes before dying out. The men stepped back into the room now carrying slabs of simmering reddish meat on a rusted baking sheet. If not for the stench and odd protrusions, the neatly cooked and cut meat would have seemed innocuous, if not appetizing. Andrew scooped the most inconspicuous slab off the tray onto a plate and carried it over to Becki, carefully placing in front of her. “We ran out of seasonings today” he said apologetically. “That’s fine” she lied, forcing a thin smile as she began to cut into the meat with a dull knife, careful not to look to closely at it as she brought a sizzling bite to her mouth. She winced with watering eyes as the sickly morsel touched her tongue, but nevertheless forced herself to chew and swallow the rancid meat. The three ate in silence, each forcing down the meal as a child does bitter medicine. Becki leaned against the wall, fighting back a wave of nausea after a particularly squishy bite had left her teeth filled with gristle. From beyond the wall she heard a sound that filled her with more dread that the demonic howls of the plagued hordes. Voices, muffled human voices. Whispers in the darkness poorly concealed. She instinctively raised a finger to her lips, using her other hand to gesture that they had hostile visitors. Joel’s face whitened as he slowly reached for his rifle and aimed it at the front door, his grip shaking with nervous anticipation. Andrew clutched his hatchet, a deep dread rising within him. Just then, the door burst inward as a group of four men in black masks armed with rifles barged into the shack, their weapons raised. Joel dropped his weapon, seeing that they were outnumbered, and put his hands in the air. “Drop your weapon, now!” one of the men commanded, aiming his rifle at Andrew, who dropped his hatchet in fear. “Please, we don’t have any supplies-“ Joel stammered. “We aren’t here for your stuff, though we’ll be taking whatever you have” he turned his focus Joel. “Search the building, take everything you find. You three come outside with me.” The three other men went to work overturning the shack as the they exited the building into the night. The sky above was a rich tapestry of stars made whole by a stunning yellow moon that shone a wealth of light to the desolate plain below. “On your knees” the main ordered, shoving Andrew to ground. He placed the barrel of his rifle to Joel’s head, his grip shaking with a palpable anger. “It took us months to track you scum down. Your leader killed my brothers, destroyed our community and brought this new Plague on us all. Never thought anyone would be stupid enough to live this close to the Plague Heart. But I guess the Heart spares it’s servants, doesn’t it?” “Please, we don’t have anything to do with Walker, he’s been dead for-“ the man collided his rifle with Joel’s head, sending him falling backward into the dead grass, a trickle of blood running down his temple. “Joel!” Becki shrieked, lunging over his side before the roar of a gunshot pierced the night and a pain unlike any she had ever felt shot up her arm. She screamed once more, falling onto her back and looking on with astonishment as Andrew leapt to his feet, charging into the man and knocking him to the ground. The rifle fell from the attacker’s hands as his tall form collided with the earth. “Run!” Andrew shouted, pushing his elbow into the man’s throat, just as the three other men burst from the shack, their weapons readied. Without hesitation, Becki leapt to her feet, adrenaline overriding the pain from her wound as she sprinted toward the away from the shack towards the cliff’s edge overlooking the plagued lands. More gunshots rang out golden bullets flew through the air, once more striking her, this time in her leg, causing her to tumble toward the ground and over the edge into the dark below.
